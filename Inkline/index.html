Inkline is a meditative knot-untangling puzzle, drawn in ink on washi paper. Drag any point on the tangled strand. Remove every crossing. When the last knot resolves, the rope settles into a sumi-e form — a mountain, a crane, a folded moon — and a vermillion seal is stamped onto your scroll.

There is no timer. No score. No streak. No ads. Nothing is chasing you.

The Garden

Every form you reveal becomes a brush stroke in a single sumi-e composition — a hanging scroll that grows with you across weeks and months. The mountains rise where you placed them. The crane flies through air you brushed open.

By the time the scroll is full, you have painted it.

The Daily Knot

One new puzzle each day, the same one for every player worldwide. A small ritual — a haiku, in tangle form. The day's micro-season is whispered above it.

No streaks to maintain. No popup if you miss one.

Drawn, not generated

Every brush stroke is hand-drawn vector calligraphy. Every sound is procedurally synthesized — a koto-like pluck on every untie, a quiet ambient bed of pentatonic notes if you want it.

No stock images. No generated art. Just ink, on paper.
