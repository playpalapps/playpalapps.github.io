Frequently asked

Still on the fence?

Does Qito work without an internet connection?
Yes — 100%. All QR generation, scanning, history, and export happens on-device. The only network calls are (a) fetching Google Fonts once on first launch, and (b) verifying a store purchase when you upgrade to Pro. Both are clearly labeled in the in-app privacy screen.
Will my QRs actually scan reliably with these custom styles?
Every preset ships with a scan-confidence meter. The painter automatically boosts error-correction when you add a logo, and the scanner runs a live check before you export. If a style isn't reliably scannable at a given payload length, Qito tells you and suggests adjustments.
What's the difference between Qito and other QR apps?
Most QR apps are either free-with-ads scanners or paid-but-generic generators. Qito is the premium tier of the generator side: hand-crafted presets, Art QR, wallpaper export, real vector SVG output, home-screen widgets, Siri shortcuts, and zero tracking. It's built like a paid Mac app — for your phone.
Can I try Pro before buying?
Yes. The Pro yearly plan includes a 7-day free trial, and you can cancel in your App Store or Play Store account at any time before the trial ends. Lifetime is a one-time purchase with no trial, refundable via the store's standard refund policy.
Does Qito support dynamic QR codes (editable after generation)?
Not in the classic "dynamic link redirector" sense — that would require a server, and we don't run one. You can always edit the source payload in Qito and regenerate the code, but the code itself is fully self-contained and scans to the exact data you encoded. That's a privacy choice: no link redirector means no tracking pixel.
Which platforms are supported?
iOS 16.0+ (iPhone and iPad) and Android 8.0+ (API 26+). The iOS build includes WidgetKit extensions and App Intents for Siri. The Android build includes the AppWidgetProvider and Material You monochrome icon.
Is my QR history backed up?
By design, no — we don't operate a cloud. Qito does give you a one-tap JSON export of your full history so you can back it up yourself, sync it between devices over AirDrop or any sharing service you trust, or re-import it after a reinstall.
